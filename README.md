
# Smoking and Health Risk Analysis Dashboard

**Created by Tahreem Shaikh**

---

## Overview

This Power BI dashboard provides a comprehensive visual analysis of smoking behavior and associated health risks across a patient population. It combines patient demographics, smoking history, and clinical indicators to surface actionable insights around cardiovascular, pulmonary, and metabolic health.

---

## Dataset

- **Total Patients:** 319
- **Average Age:** 54.9
- **Average BMI:** 29.8

---

## Dashboard Components

### 1. % of Smoking Status (Donut Chart)
Breaks down the patient population into three smoking categories:
- **Never:** 152 patients (47.65%)
- **Former:** 98 patients (30.72%)
- **Current:** 69 patients (21.63%)

### 2. Smoking Status by Gender (Grouped Bar Chart)
Compares male and female distribution across Never, Current, and Former smoker groups. Highlights gender-based differences in smoking prevalence.

### 3. Smoking Duration & Daily Intake (Line Chart)
Tracks two metrics across age groups (29–38 to 69+):
- **YOS** — Years of Smoking
- **CPD** — Cigarettes Per Day

Reveals how smoking intensity and duration shift with age.

### 4. Cholesterol & Hypertension Risk by Age Group (Stacked Bar Chart)
Displays the proportion of High, Low, and Normal cholesterol/hypertension risk levels across age bands (18–28 to 69+). Helps identify which age groups carry the greatest cardiovascular burden.

### 5. Organ Health Toggle (Damaged / Healthy)
An interactive anatomical visual that allows toggling between **Healthy** and **Damaged** states to illustrate the physiological impact of smoking on major organs — Heart, Lungs, Liver, and Kidneys.

### 6. Organ Sidebar Navigation
Quick-filter panel on the left allows drilling into organ-specific views:
- Heart
- Lungs
- Kidney
- Liver

---

## Tools Used

- **Power BI Desktop** — dashboard design and visualization
- **Dataset** — publically available patient health and smoking records

---

## Key Insights

- Nearly half the patient population has never smoked, but over 50% have a smoking history (current or former).
- Cigarettes per day and years of smoking peak in mid-age groups and decline among older patients, possibly due to cessation or survivorship bias.
- High cholesterol and hypertension risk is notably elevated in the 39–68 age range.
- Male patients outnumber female patients in both the Current and Former smoker categories.

---

## How to Use

1. Open the `.pbix` file in **Power BI Desktop**.
2. Use the **organ sidebar** on the left to filter by organ system.
3. Toggle between **Damaged** and **Healthy** views in the anatomical panel.
4. Hover over charts for detailed tooltips on individual data points.
